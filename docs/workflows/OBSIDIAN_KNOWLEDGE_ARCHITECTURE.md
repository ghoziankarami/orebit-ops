# Obsidian Knowledge Architecture

This document defines the intended knowledge architecture for the Obsidian vault used by Orebit operations.

## Goal

The vault should become the integrated second brain and source of truth for:

- research
- literature and paper synthesis
- geology and exploration knowledge
- mining and offshore operational knowledge
- product-digital knowledge
- SOPs, workflows, and reusable systems

## Layer model

### `0. Inbox/`
Raw intake and low-conflict capture surfaces.

### `1. Projects/`
Active work with a finish line.

### `2. Areas/`
Ongoing stewardship and systems.

### `3. Resources/`
Durable reusable knowledge.

### `4. Archive/`
Inactive or legacy material.

- `3. Resources/Visual Concepts/`
- `3. Resources/Product Knowledge/`
- `3. Resources/Prompt Packs/`
- `3. Resources/Operating Systems/`

## Intake lanes

Low-conflict intake should land in:
- `0. Inbox/Research/`
- `0. Inbox/Automation Inbox/`
- `0. Inbox/Papers/`
- other existing inbox subfolders when they are the natural destination

## Product-digital lane

Active builds should live under `1. Projects/Product Studio/`.
Reusable learnings should be promoted into resource lanes.

## Research lane

Active research programs should live under `1. Projects/Research Programs/`.
Promoted research outputs should live in `3. Resources/Research Notes/` and adjacent durable lanes.

For legacy-but-important research lineages, prefer a compact project package in `1. Projects/Research Programs/` rather than a single giant note or a raw runtime dump.
That package can then feed durable promotions into `3. Resources/...`.

## Legacy interpretation

Older runtime folders such as `nala` and `research-data` may still matter historically, but the canonical readable knowledge base going forward is the Obsidian vault itself.
